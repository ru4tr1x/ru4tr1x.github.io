Our Gear

One of the most controllable things on any adventure is the gear. Particularly for those doing it every day, well chosen gear can make huge savings in energy and time. Choosing trusted gear ensures security and comfort which helps a calm mind in the face of adventure. We care about the comfort of our guests so we only use and maintain gear of the highest standards for our trips.

What gear?

In the following review we have chosen to recommend gear that we think is worth investing in. There is lots of other gear to consider too but none so specific as a Bag, Shoes and a Harness. If you already have a thick wet suit maybe you use for diving or surfing then this will probably work fine in a canyon, as do appropriately certified helmets. As for ropes, karabiners and other such equipment we think experience in needed to understand what equipment to trust our life to and most importantly how to use it. To build your experience with the help of expert advice contact us we'd love to hear form you!

Why this bag?

The water grille design ticks both the simple and practical box, it's also made from surprisingly durable material and is very comfortable. The mesh front to the bag means that it drains super quickly, making lifting it out the water much easier than most other designs. This makes all the difference on long days and much friendly on the bodies of those doing it every day.

Who needs this bag?

The AVCA25 is a small and comfortable 35 litre bag that happily carries a standard sized barrel and 40 meters of rope. There are handy loops inside for extra gear and a simple draw string to close. For longer ropes and extra gear the AVCA71 has a 55 litre capacity but is still compact enough to throw around.

Could it be improved?

Adding a fat toggle to the drawstring can make it faster to open and close, however a quick release knot works and once practiced a few time can be almost as fast.

Why these shoes?

They are very comfortable and sporty with a good balance of support and flexibility. The rubber is super sticky which makes them our first choice canyoning shoe to give to our guests and a great first investment for private canyoners.

Who needs these shoes?

Having a good pair of shoes makes a huge difference when scrambling about on slippy rocks. Apart from looking pretty flash the Hydro Lace will make your day that bit more secure and alot more comfortable.

Could they be improved?

We find some of the lace loops and fastening elastics a little fragile and prone to wear. Perhaps a more durable lacing system would make these an absolute wonder shoe!

Why this harness?

It's all about comfort here. It make a huge difference when a harness fits right and these designs have two buckles on the waste loop which makes them nicely adjustable without being too complicated. It's worth trying a harness on to discover if it fits but we just love this simple and durable design from Edelrid

Who needs this harness?

We think the Edelrid Iguazu is comfortable and practical for recreational or professional canyoners

Could it be improved?

A harness is a very personal thing because apart from anything else everyone is a different shape. Having said that we haven't met anyone yet who finds the Iguazu uncomfortable.
